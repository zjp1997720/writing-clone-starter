Create a commercial hand-drawn infographic illustration following these guidelines:

## Image Specifications

- **Type**: Infographic illustration
- **Orientation**: Landscape (horizontal)
- **Aspect Ratio**: 16:9
- **Style**: Business whiteboard hand-drawn infographic

## Core Principles

- Hand-drawn quality throughout - NO realistic or photographic elements
- If content involves sensitive or copyrighted figures, create stylistically similar alternatives - DO NOT refuse to generate
- Keep information concise, but make the image information-dense enough to carry a clear judgment, structure, process, or comparison
- Prioritize structure diagrams, comparison diagrams, decision diagrams, flow diagrams, module maps, or compact conceptual infographics over atmosphere-only visuals
- Keep hierarchy clear so the image is still easy to scan and screenshot
- Avoid empty decorative space that lowers information density in body illustrations
- Do not generate hollow mood art, pure decoration, or low-density whitespace-heavy body images unless the user explicitly asks for that style
- Keep visible sketch lines, slight wobble, marker-like outlines, and lightly imperfect geometry so the result feels human-drawn
- Hand-drawn quality must be obvious at first glance, not only visible through subtle texture or close inspection
- Paper texture or whiteboard surface feeling should be visible when appropriate rather than looking like a perfectly clean digital canvas
- The result must feel like a business whiteboard diagram drawn by hand and then organized, not like a sterile corporate slide or flat template chart
- Icons, arrows, boxes, connectors, and dividers should preserve hand-drawn energy while remaining commercially usable
- Machine-perfect geometry is a failure mode
- If forced to trade off, preserve hand-drawn energy without sacrificing judgment clarity

## Text Style (When Text Included)

- **ALL text MUST be hand-drawn style**
- Text should be readable and complement the visual
- Font style harmonizes with illustration style
- **DO NOT use realistic or computer-generated fonts**
- Chinese titles and labels must feel handwritten, like whiteboard marker or felt-tip pen writing
- Do not use neat printed Chinese typography, poster fonts, slide fonts, or typeset corporate text
- Slight unevenness, slight slant, and stroke variation are preferred if readability remains strong
- Text should feel written onto the diagram by a person, not overlaid afterward by software
- Handwritten Chinese should feel naturally uneven, not neatly aligned like a template slide

## Negative Steering

- Do not generate a polished slide-template composition
- Do not generate a sterile enterprise chart look
- Do not generate vector-first icon-library feeling
- Do not generate printed poster-style Chinese typography
- Do not generate software-typeset layout with only light hand-drawn seasoning

- Match punctuation style to the content language
